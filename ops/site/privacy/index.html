Privacy policy

Distavo keeps meeting content under your control.

Distavo is designed so that your recordings and notes stay on machines you control. This page describes what data Distavo produces, where it is stored, and what leaves your Mac.

What data Distavo produces

For each recording it processes, Distavo creates:

  • A WAV copy of the recording, produced locally by AVFoundation from your original file.
  • A transcript of the audio, returned by your WhisperX server.
  • A summary or structured note, produced by your Ollama model from the transcript.
  • The original recording file remains where you placed it.

Where data is stored locally

  • Recordings: the watched folder, by default ~/Documents/Distavo/recordings.
  • Notes: by default ~/Documents/Distavo/notes/<name>.md.
  • Working files and configuration: under ~/Library/Application Support/Distavo/.
  • Logs: ~/Library/Logs/Distavo/distavo.log.

These files are written in cleartext under your home directory. Distavo does not encrypt them; protect them with your normal macOS account and disk encryption, such as FileVault.

What leaves your Mac

Distavo talks to exactly two network endpoints, both of which you configure:

  • Your WhisperX server, which receives the locally converted WAV for transcription.
  • Your Ollama server, which receives the cleaned transcript for summarisation.

These endpoints can be on localhost, your own network, or a remote machine. Because audio and transcript text leave your Mac to reach them, only configure servers you operate or fully trust.

No telemetry

Distavo has no telemetry and no phone-home. It does not collect analytics, crash reports, usage statistics, or any other data, and it does not contact any server other than the WhisperX and Ollama URLs you set.

Recommendation

Prefer local or otherwise trusted WhisperX and Ollama servers. Review the configured URLs before processing sensitive recordings.
